The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is India's national space agency, established in 1969, responsible for developing space technology for national needs like communication, remote sensing, and navigation, alongside deep space exploration, including landmark missions to the Moon (Chandrayaan) and Mars (MOM), with a focus on human spaceflight (Gaganyaan) and astronomy (Aditya-L1, XPoSat). Headquartered in Bengaluru, it operates numerous centers across India and is known for its cost-effective, cutting-edge space science and applications.

National Development: Applies space tech for communication, TV broadcasting, weather forecasting, resource management, and navigation.
Space Exploration: Conducts scientific missions like Chandrayaan (Moon) and Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM).
Technology Development: Builds satellites, launch vehicles (rockets), and ground systems for various space activities.
Research: Focuses on astronomy, astrophysics, planetary sciences, and atmospheric sciences.
