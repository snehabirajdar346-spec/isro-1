ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation) conducts diverse space missions, from satellite launches for communication/earth observation (like INSAT, IRS, EOS series) to landmark planetary explorations such as Chandrayaan (Moon) and Mangalyaan (Mars), and solar observation with Aditya-L1. Key recent successes include Chandrayaan-3's lunar south pole landing, while future plans involve the human spaceflight program Gaganyaan, Venus orbiter, and lunar sample return missions. Key Completed Missions

Chandrayaan (Lunar Exploration):

Chandrayaan-1 (2008): First Indian lunar mission, discovered water molecules on the Moon.
Chandrayaan-2 (2019): Orbiter successful; lander had a partial failure, but orbiter continues.
Chandrayaan-3 (2023): Achieved India's first soft landing on the lunar south pole, making India the first nation to do so.
Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan - 2013): India's first interplanetary mission, successfully reaching Mars orbit.
Aditya-L1 (2023): India's first dedicated solar mission, studying the Sun from the L1 Lagrange point.
XPoSat (2024): First dedicated astronomy mission for X-ray polarization studies.
Satellite Constellations: Extensive series for communication (GSAT), navigation (IRNSS/NavIC), and Earth observation (IRS, EOS).
Major Planned & Future Missions.

Gaganyaan:

India's human spaceflight program, with crewed and uncrewed test flights planned.
NISAR (NASA-ISRO Synthetic Aperture Radar): Joint mission with NASA for dual-band radar imaging.
Chandrayaan-4 / Lunar Polar Exploration Mission: A lunar sample-return mission.
Venus Orbiter Mission: An orbiter to study Venus's atmosphere.
Mars Lander Mission: A follow-up to MOM, including a lander/rover.
SPADEX (Space-based Plasma Depletion Experiment): Demonstrating technology for space debris mitigation.
