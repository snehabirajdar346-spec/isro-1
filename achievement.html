ISRO's major achievements include developing indigenous launch vehicles (PSLV, GSLV) for cost-effective satellite deployment, pioneering affordable interplanetary missions like Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) and Chandrayaan, discovering water on the Moon with Chandrayaan-1, setting records for launching numerous satellites (104 in one go), providing vital services (NavIC, INSAT), and advancing towards human spaceflight (Gaganyaan) and solar observation (Aditya-L1), making India a leading space power. Key Milestones & Missions: Aryabhata (1975): India's first satellite, marking the start of its space journey. SLV-3 (1980): First indigenous launch vehicle, making India the 7th nation with orbital launch capability. INSAT System: One of the world's largest domestic communication satellite networks, crucial for telecom, weather, and broadcasting. Chandrayaan-1 (2008): Discovered water molecules on the Moon. Mangalyaan (MOM, 2013): India became the first nation to reach Mars orbit on its first attempt, very cost-effectively. PSLV Record (2017): Launched 104 satellites in a single mission. Chandrayaan-3 (2023): Soft-landed near the lunar south pole, making India the first country to do so. Aditya-L1 (2023/2024): India's first solar observatory mission, reached its L1 halo orbit.

    Indigenous Capabilities:
    Launch Vehicles: Developed reliable PSLV, GSLV, and GSLV Mk-III (LVM3) for diverse satellite and deep-space launches.
    Cryogenic Engines: One of six agencies with full launch capability, including cryogenic engines.
    Navigation: Created the regional NavIC (IRNSS) system for independent navigation.
    Socio-Economic Impact: Space tech used for disaster management, resource monitoring, telecommunications, and education.
    Gaganyaan: Preparing for India's first human spaceflight mission.
    Reusable Rockets: Successfully tested reusable launch vehicle (RLV) technology.
